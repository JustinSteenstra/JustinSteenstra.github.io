AerospaceNU Propulsion Subgroup

AerospaceNU propulsion subgroup is designing a liquid rocket engine using kerosene and LOX that will be attached to a rocket that will compete in the dollar-per-foot challenge. In this group we are designing, manufactureing, and building the engine along with a horizontal and vertical test stand. The result will be an ablative engine with a machined triplet-impinging fuel injector.

NU London Robotics

NU London Robotics is the first robotics club at Northeastern London created by me and 3 others. We created a fully autonomous robot that was used in the Unibots UK competition hosted by the University of Cambridge. The robot was made to autonmously navigate a square arena against 3 other robots and collect ping pong balls and deliver them to specific team desingated boxes to earn points.

Life of a Potato Museum Exhibit

Life of a Potato is a museum exhbit project that was created to teach proffessors and students about resilient engineering in regards to agriculture. In this project our team of 4 designed and created an interactive learning game that included a visual display, LEDS, and arcade buttons. We also created a poster board with extra information and integrated leds within it to show the progress the player makes through the game. In addition, we laser cut and assembled model hydroponic farms using acrylic to increase the users learning. Overall our team met all deadlines and got 2nd place overall in educational resiliency.

Pasta Bridge

In this project our team of four created 2 pasta bridges with various different types of pasta under a 120 gram weight constraint. We created a segemented arch bridge and a rounded arch bridge and tested their weight holding capacity. They ended up holding 7 and 5 pounds, respectively.
