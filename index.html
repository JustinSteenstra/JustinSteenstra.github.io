About Me

Hello! I am a senior Mechanical Engineering student at Northeastern University and I will graduate in December 2025. I am currently apart of AeroNu, Northeastern's rocketry team, and I have completed one Co-op at Barletta Engineering Heavy Division gaining 6 months of work experience.

At Barletta I worked as a project engineer working on two main projects. Managing the improvements of Boston's red line train maintenance facility and a Bridge bundle project. On both these jobs I worked with the Massachusetts bay transit authority (MBTA), contractors, construction workers, and other engineers to ensure was completed on time and within budget as per the contract. I also reviewed project schedules, submitted RFI's and submittals, reviewed shop drawings, and compiled change orders.

Since I was a kid I have always liked building and fixing things whether it was erector sets, legos, and working on car projects like brakes with my dad. This same passion is with me today but now with other engineers working on projects at Northeastern. Thus, I am looking forward to working in a full time position where the projects that I work on with others will help progress the human race and change the world for the better.

Resiliant Agriculture Museum Exhibit

Life of a Potato is a museum exhbit project that was created to teach proffessors and students about resilient engineering in regards to agriculture. In this project our team of 4 designed and created an interactive learning game that included a visual display, LEDS, and arcade buttons. We also created a poster board with extra information and integrated leds within it to show the progress the player makes through the game. In addition, we laser cut and assembled model hydroponic farms using acrylic to increase the users learning. Overall our team met all deadlines and got 2nd place overall in educational resiliency.

Anaylsis of Blood and Water Collision with Wall

As my final project for my CFD class I analyzed the collision of blood with a wall using Ansys Fluent. I first created a 2d model in Ansys Fluent of a nozzle and chamber with a wall and used the volume of fluid method to track the interface between air and blood. Then I created used the viscosity user defined function to create a function for the blood viscosity in order to analyze the fluid as blood. Then I ran the simulation at various time steps to anaylze the progession of the collision of blood with a wall.
